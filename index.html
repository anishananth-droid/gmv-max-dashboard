Insights & Recommendations

H1 accelerated hard — revenue up 73.7% quarter-over-quarter

Apr–Jun did RM1,260,822 at 19.3x ROI versus Jan–Mar's RM725,996 at 12.2x. Orders grew 80.5% and cost-per-order fell 39.2% over the same split — this reads as broad efficiency improvement, not one lucky month.

One retired campaign drove over half of H1 revenue

"Product GMV Max" (legacy) generated RM1,003,193 — 50.5% of the entire H1 total — before being retired when the account restructured into today's category campaigns around May 8. Any run-rate comparison against full-H1 totals needs to account for this, or it will misread the restructure as a slowdown.

LIVE GMV Max is the strongest campaign standing today

RM332,812 revenue at 16.82x ROI across 1,557 orders — comfortably ahead of the next-largest current-structure campaign, All Mens (1), at RM203,995. Between the two, they're the backbone of the post-restructure account.

January was the soft point of H1

RM75,865 revenue at just 6.15x ROI — the only month under 10x all year. Every month since cleared 13x, and May/June both topped 18x. Worth understanding what was different structurally in January before planning next year's Q1.

Men's core denim dominates the H1 top 10

501 Original, 511 Slim Fit (two SKUs), 512 Slim Taper, and 505 Rinse/Ama Rinsey fill 7 of the top 10 product slots by revenue. Only two women's styles — both 318 Wide-Leg variants — break into the list.

CTR is lower and tighter across the H1 top 10 than the recent 30-day view

6.20%–7.13% here, versus 6.9%–8.4% in the early-July snapshot — though these H1 figures are scoped to each product's single dominant (mostly legacy) campaign rather than every campaign it ran in, so treat them as directional rather than an exact account-wide rate.

Two top-revenue products are close to selling out

Women's Trucker Jacket has 7 units left and 505 Regular Fit 0260 has 13 — this reflects live TikTok Shop inventory as of this pull, not the H1 date range, but both are still active top performers today.

Promotion-days automation is still off on LIVE GMV Max

Live campaign config check: LIVE GMV Max's promotion-days auto-budget feature remains disabled, and its 15x bid target still sits below TikTok's account-recommended 16.6x — the same gap flagged in the last snapshot, unchanged.

01 / INVESTIGATE

Understand January's soft performance before H2 planning

6.15x ROI was the outlier low of H1. Confirm whether it was catalog breadth, budget caps, seasonality, or something structural — it's the one month that doesn't fit the otherwise steady climb to 18x+.

02 / DOCUMENT THE RESTRUCTURE

Flag the May 8 campaign restructure in any historical reporting

"Product GMV Max" alone was 50.5% of H1 revenue and no longer exists. Any YoY or run-rate comparison built on campaign names or campaign-level history needs an explicit note, or it will look like performance collapsed rather than migrated.

03 / PROTECT STOCK

Flag low-stock winners to merchandising now

Trucker Jacket (7 units) and 505 Regular Fit 0260 (13 units) are both still-active top performers. Ad spend on a product that sells out mid-flight is wasted spend — loop in inventory before it happens.

04 / ENABLE PROMOTION-DAYS

Turn on promotion-days automation for LIVE GMV Max

Still off, still your top current-structure campaign. TikTok is already delivering 11–23% estimated uplift from this exact feature on three smaller campaigns — this remains a one-click change with no downside case made against it yet.

Data gaps & caveats — read before presenting
  • Campaign restructure mid-H1. The account replaced a small set of broad campaigns (including the RM1M-revenue "Product GMV Max") with today's category/SKU-dedicated structure around May 8, 2026. All 16 campaigns with any H1 spend are included in the campaign chart, with legacy ones labeled accordingly.
  • Monthly, not daily, trend granularity. TikTok's API caps daily-dimension (stat_time_day) queries at a 30-day range per call. Covering all of H1 at daily granularity would need roughly 7 chunked calls × 16 campaigns; monthly aggregation was used instead as the appropriate resolution for a 6-month view.
  • Day-of-week uses the most recent 30 days as a proxy, not all of H1 — recomputing a true H1-wide day-of-week pattern would require the same expensive daily-chunking above, multiplied across 6 months. Treat it as a recent-behavior snapshot, not an H1-wide average.
  • Clicks/impressions are scoped to each product's single dominant campaign, not summed across every campaign it ran in during H1 (unlike cost/revenue/orders/ROI, which are true full-H1 totals). This is why the Clicks KPI tile is labeled separately from the other five, true-total KPIs.
  • Impressions and clicks exist, but only at product-post level. TikTok's GMV Max API only returns product_impressions and product_clicks when queried per individual campaign + product pair, never as an account or campaign-wide rollup.
  • LIVE GMV Max reports ~0 product impressions/clicks even for products generating real revenue through it — livestream conversions aren't attributed through the same product-card tracking as static product posts.
  • No ad-group/ad-level structure. GMV Max has no manual ad groups or ads; product (SKU-group) and creative/video IDs are the closest analogs.
  • Cost vs. net cost. Campaigns are enrolled in TikTok's ROI-protection compensation program, so net cost can run below gross cost once compensation posts, with a lag of a week or more. This dashboard uses gross cost for ROI/CPO throughout for consistency; net cost is shown separately for transparency.
  • Scope. This account also runs one unrelated, zero-spend "Community interaction" auction campaign outside GMV Max, excluded here as out of scope.
  • Stock and campaign-config data is a live snapshot, pulled separately from the H1 performance figures above (which are historical). Quantities, promotion-days status, and bid targets reflect account state at the time of this pull, not a trend over H1.
