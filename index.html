Insights & Recommendations

Order volume is the real headline, not ROI

Orders fell 20.5% week-over-week while spend fell only 13.3% — ROI looks like it improved (+9.8%) but that's arithmetic, not health. Fewer, pricier conversions are pushing the ratio up while overall demand cools.

LIVE GMV Max is the single biggest lever

LIVE GMV Max_Levi's Malaysia alone is 36% of spend and 38% of revenue (RM193,621 at 19.2x ROI) — livestream-driven GMV Max is outperforming every static product campaign at scale.

Spend is heavily concentrated

The top 2 campaigns absorb 65% of all spend; top 3 absorb 76%. The remaining 7 active campaigns share the rest, and 6 more campaigns sit disabled at RM0 — worth a cleanup pass.

Saturday spend spikes, but efficiency drops

Saturday averages RM1,554/day spend — 80%+ above other weekdays — yet posts the lowest ROI of the week (14.9x). This is driven largely by a single anomaly: a RM2,258 spend spike on Jun 6 (a "6.6" mega-sale day) that pulled Saturday's average down.

Several underspent products are highly efficient

318 Shaping Wide-Leg 0001 (50.6x ROI), 0014 (49.0x), and the Women's Trucker Jacket (47.1x) are all running on under RM300 of spend each — real headroom to scale before efficiency likely decays.

512 Slim Taper Jeans lags the top-10 product set

At 10.1x ROI on RM1,013 spend, it's the weakest ROI among the top 10 products by revenue — worth a creative or targeting review relative to its RM319 price point peers.

01 / REALLOCATE

Shift incremental budget to underspent, high-ROI products

318 Wide-Leg 0001/0014, the Trucker Jacket, and 315 Bootcut are all >39x ROI on <RM300 spend. Test doubling their budget before assuming diminishing returns.

02 / INVESTIGATE

Diagnose the order-volume decline before celebrating the ROI gain

Confirm whether the 20.5% WoW order drop is post-6.6-sale hangover, seasonal, or creative fatigue on LIVE GMV Max and All Mens (1) — the two campaigns carrying 65% of spend.

03 / CLEAN UP

Smooth mega-sale spend spikes and retire dead campaigns

Cap single-day spend surges like Jun 6 to avoid the efficiency dip seen on high-spend Saturdays, and archive the 6 disabled, zero-spend campaigns cluttering reporting.

Data gaps & caveats — read before presenting
  • No impressions, clicks, CTR, CPC, CPM, or reach exist for GMV Max at any level. This is a platform limitation of TikTok's GMV Max Reporting API (an automated, catalog-driven product), not a partial pull — confirmed by direct API testing, not assumed.
  • No ad-group/ad-level structure. GMV Max has no manual ad groups or ads; product (SKU-group) and creative/video IDs are the closest analogs. The "ad creative" ranking requested was built from product-level data instead.
  • Day-of-week, not hour-of-day. Hourly breakdowns require one API call per single day (30 calls for this window); day-of-week uses the full 30-day pull already retrieved and is fully real.
  • Cost vs. net cost. All active campaigns are enrolled in TikTok's ROI-protection compensation program, so net cost runs ~11% below gross cost. This dashboard uses gross cost for ROI/CPO throughout for consistency; net cost is shown separately for transparency.
  • Scope. This account also runs one unrelated, zero-spend "Community interaction" auction campaign outside GMV Max, excluded here as out of scope.
